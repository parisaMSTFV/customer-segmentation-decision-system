# Segmentation Dimensions

The segmentation system integrates five independent analytical dimensions, each designed to capture a distinct aspect of customer behavior and value.

---

## 1. Demographics

Purpose: Understand structural customer differences.

Sample features:
- City tier / population density
- Financial indicators (proxy-based)
- Location clustering

Business relevance:
Supports geographic targeting and pricing sensitivity analysis.

---

## 2. Transactional

Purpose: Capture monetary contribution and purchasing behavior.

Sample features:
- Order frequency
- Revenue contribution
- Margin contribution
- Repeat purchase cycle
- Category concentration

Business relevance:
Identifies high-value and low-value customers for investment prioritization.

---

## 3. Behavioral

Purpose: Measure engagement and interaction depth.

Sample features:
- Product views
- Add-to-cart behavior
- Engagement rate
- Session activity signals
- Plus activity days

Business relevance:
Enables personalization and early churn signal detection.

---

## 4. Psychographics

Purpose: Capture preference patterns and intent signals.

Sample features:
- Favorite categories
- Observed product clusters
- Purchase chain patterns
- Time-based engagement behavior

Business relevance:
Supports persona discovery and personalization strategies.

---

## 5. CX & Feedback

Purpose: Integrate satisfaction and experience signals.

Sample features:
- NPS
- Product ratings
- Comment patterns
- Return / cancel behavior

Business relevance:
Aligns segmentation with experience quality and retention risk.
